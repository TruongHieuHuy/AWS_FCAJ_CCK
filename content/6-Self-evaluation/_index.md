During my internship at Amazon Web Services Vietnam LLC (from May 4, 2026, to July 25, 2026), I had the opportunity to rub shoulders and apply my academic knowledge within the most rigorous practical working environment.

Rather than just playing a peripheral "support" role, I was directly involved in designing and deploying the **Budget Tracker** project – a smart personal finance management system. I personally set up the core Serverless architecture flows using AWS Lambda and Amazon DynamoDB, while deeply integrating services like Amazon Cognito for identity management and Amazon CloudWatch for real-time system monitoring. This experience not only honed my programming skills but also elevated my systemic thinking and architectural decision-making capabilities.

Regarding professional ethics, I strictly adhered to the organization's security regulations, completed tasks within SLAs, and continuously synced up with colleagues to ensure the project's seamless progress.

### 🧠 Areas for Improvement

Looking at the project through the lens of a "T-shaped Engineer," I realize I need to optimize the following areas:

- 🛠️ **Technical Perspective (Cloud Engineer):** I need to delve deeper into Infrastructure as Code skills (using Terraform or AWS CDK) to fully automate infrastructure deployment (Deployment Automation). While manual configuration via the Console is good for initial learning, it becomes a bottleneck when the project needs to scale or ensure reproducibility in actual CI/CD environments.
- 💡 **Business & System Perspective (BA/SA):** I recognize the need to further refine my cross-functional communication skills. Specifically, the ability to "translate" complex technical risks (e.g., the root causes of Lambda timeouts or DynamoDB read/write capacity limits) into easily understandable business language, so that non-technical Stakeholders can grasp the situation and make timely decisions.
